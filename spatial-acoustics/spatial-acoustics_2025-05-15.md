The fifth research strand examines the relationship between sound, space, and light in the context of responsive sculpture. While the sensor integration work treats ambient sound as one input channel among several, this strand focuses specifically on how the acoustic properties of a gallery space shape — and can be shaped by — the behavior of light sculptures.

Questions driving this investigation:

- How do the acoustic characteristics of different gallery spaces ([reverb time](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reverberation#RT60), resonant frequencies, ambient noise floor) influence the quality of sound-to-light mapping?
- Can a sculpture's light behavior make the acoustic properties of a space *visible* to viewers?
- Is there an aesthetic correspondence between spatial acoustics and light patterns that feels intuitive rather than arbitrary?

This strand draws on my earlier experience with architectural acoustics and the growing field of audiovisual synesthesia research.
