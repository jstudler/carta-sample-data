Today's experiment: casting NP-3012 addressable LED strips into clear epoxy resin to create self-illuminating sculptural panels.

Process:

  • Cut LED strips into 20cm segments (12 LEDs each)
  • Soldered data and power connections with silicone-insulated wire for heat resistance
  • Suspended strips in a silicone mold at 5mm depth
  • Poured two-part epoxy resin (ClearCast) in 3mm layers to avoid exothermic cracking
  • Cured for 48 hours at room temperature

Results are promising. The resin diffuses the individual LED points into a smooth glow, which is exactly the effect I want — you see light emanating from within the material rather than discrete point sources. The warm tone of the resin adds a slight amber cast that works beautifully with cool blue and white LEDs but muddies warm colors.

Problem: the resin transmits heat poorly, so the LEDs run about 15°C hotter than in open air. At full brightness, this causes color shift (blues become greener) and may reduce LED lifespan. Need to limit maximum brightness to about 60% or find a resin with better thermal conductivity.
