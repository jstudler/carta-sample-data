--- title: Conclusion — Projection Mapping date: 2025-09-05 topic: projection-mapping category: studio-work type: conclusion width: 3 ---

After five months of experimentation with both embedded LEDs and projected light, clear conclusions have emerged about the role of each technique in my practice.

Embedded LEDs in translucent resin are the primary technique for the portable, self-contained sculptures that form the core of this project. The resin-diffused glow is visually distinctive and materially coherent — the light feels like a property of the sculpture itself rather than something applied to it. The addition of diffusion film (Lumiveil D2) inside CNC-milled TGMA shells solved the hotspot problem and opens up more complex three-dimensional forms.

Projected light remains valuable for site-specific work and for early prototyping of light patterns before committing to embedded hardware. The projection mapping experiments also revealed the aesthetic potential of viewer shadows as an unplanned interaction modality.

Key technical achievements:

  • Developed a resin casting process that reliably embeds LED strips with less than 5% failure rate
  • Established thermal limits: maximum 60% brightness for continuous operation in 8mm resin
  • Created a library of 12 parametric light patterns driven by the sensor platform
  • Successfully exhibited a prototype at two public events

The tension between transparency and enchantment identified during the open studio event is a rich area for continued reflection.
