The material studies strand resolved the primary question of which substrates are suitable for embedding LEDs in sculptural forms, while opening up an unexpected aesthetic direction with the washi paper technique.

## Confirmed Materials

- **ClearCast (clear epoxy)** — the standard material for small to medium panels (up to 30cm). Reliable diffusion, acceptable thermal performance at ≤60% brightness, no aging issues over 4 weeks.
- **TGMA** — the preferred material for larger [CNC-milled](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Numerical_control) forms. Better [thermal conductivity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thermal_conductivity), neutral color, requires a UV clear coat to prevent scratching.
- **Lumiveil D2 diffusion film** — an effective supplement when material thickness alone does not provide sufficient diffusion.

## Open Direction

The washi paper diffusion technique remains the most visually compelling finding from this strand, but its long-term durability is unproven. I have archived three test castings for a 12-month observation period. If they hold up, this technique could become central to a future body of work that embraces material unpredictability as an aesthetic principle.

## Practical Impact

The material knowledge developed here directly informed the fabrication of the exhibition pieces shown at Galerie Meridian. The combination of ClearCast shells at 8mm thickness with Lumiveil D2 diffusion film became the standard construction for all portable pieces in this project.
