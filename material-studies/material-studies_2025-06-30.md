Completed the four-week accelerated aging test on the ClearCast and TGMA samples. Both were run at 80% LED brightness continuously for 28 days in a controlled 22°C environment.

## ClearCast Results

- No visible yellowing (unlike the polyester resin which yellowed within 14 days in the earlier test)
- Measured a 0.4% decrease in [light transmission](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transmittance) at 550nm — within measurement error
- Surface hardness unchanged
- One LED in the test strip failed on day 19, likely due to sustained thermal stress. The surrounding resin showed no damage

## TGMA Results

- No color change
- Light transmission unchanged
- Minor surface scratching from handling — TGMA is softer than acrylic and will need a protective coating for exhibition pieces

## Conclusion

Both materials are viable for exhibitions lasting up to 8 weeks at 80% brightness. The LED failure in the resin sample reinforces the earlier decision to limit continuous brightness to 60% for embedded applications. TGMA's scratch sensitivity is manageable with a UV-cured clear coat.
