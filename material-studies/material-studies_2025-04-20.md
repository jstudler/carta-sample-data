The fourth strand of this research investigates the material substrates for kinetic light sculpture. Light does not exist in isolation — it requires a surface or volume to become visible. The choice of material profoundly shapes the aesthetic character of a responsive light work.

Key questions:

- Which [translucent materials](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transparency_and_translucency) best diffuse LED light while maintaining structural integrity?
- How do different materials age under continuous LED illumination?
- What is the relationship between material thickness, light diffusion quality, and thermal performance?

This topic encompasses bench tests, material sourcing, and iterative prototyping of sculptural shells designed to house embedded LED systems.
