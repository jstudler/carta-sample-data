Conducted a side-by-side comparison of four translucent casting materials, each poured into identical 100mm × 100mm × 8mm slab molds with a strip of 6 NP-3012 LEDs embedded at 4mm depth.

## Materials Tested

1. **ClearCast (clear epoxy)** — slight warm amber tint, excellent diffusion, long cure time (48h). Running temperature at 60% LED brightness: +14°C above ambient.
2. **Polyester casting resin** — water-clear when fresh, yellows within 2 weeks under LED illumination. Cheaper but unsuitable for long-term installations.
3. **TGMA (thermoformed sheet)** — neutral color, moderate diffusion, can be CNC-milled. Runs cooler (+8°C) due to better thermal conductivity.
4. **Frosted acrylic** — excellent diffusion, no color shift, but cannot embed LEDs inside — requires surface-mounting behind the sheet.

## Findings

The ClearCast remains the best compromise for embedded applications despite its thermal limitations. TGMA is the stronger candidate for larger forms where CNC milling is required. The polyester resin is eliminated from further consideration due to yellowing.

An interesting discovery: layering a thin sheet of Japanese [washi paper](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Washi) between the LED strip and the outer resin surface produces a beautifully organic diffusion pattern with visible fiber texture. Worth exploring further.
