Ran a full calibration session in the studio today, simulating gallery conditions as closely as possible. Key findings:

  • The proximity sensor needs to be recalibrated for each installation because reflective surfaces (polished floors, glass walls) create false readings.
  • Temperature sensors near LED arrays read 2-3°C higher than ambient due to heat from the LEDs themselves. Need to either shield the sensor or compensate in software.
  • The microphone AGC (automatic gain control) causes problems — in a quiet gallery, the gain ramps up until the ventilation hum dominates. Switched to manual gain with a calibration routine that samples the room for 60 seconds on startup.

Also discovered that The I2C bus occasionally hangs when the proximity sensor is at maximum range. Added a watchdog timer that resets the bus after 500ms of silence. This is the kind of robustness issue that only surfaces during extended testing.

Discussed the calibration challenges with Dr. Nils Erhardt at the university's embedded systems lab. He suggested using a Kalman filter for the proximity channel, which could handle the reflective surface issue more elegantly. Worth exploring.
