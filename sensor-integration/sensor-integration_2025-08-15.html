--- title: Conclusion — Sensor Integration date: 2025-08-15 topic: sensor-integration category: studio-work type: conclusion width: 3 ---

After five months of prototyping, testing, and iterating, the sensor integration strand has reached a stable plateau. The final sensor platform consists of four environmental channels (temperature, humidity, ambient sound, proximity) running on a compact Luvon Micro + Crestboard stack.

Key conclusions:

  • The most aesthetically productive sensor is, surprisingly, temperature. Its slowness forces a contemplative pace that aligns well with the sculptural medium.
  • Proximity sensing is essential for creating a sense of dialogue between viewer and sculpture, but must be carefully calibrated to avoid binary on/off behavior.
  • Ambient sound is the most difficult parameter to use well. Averaging it over 5-10 seconds strips out the transient character that makes it interesting, but shorter windows produce nervous, distracting light behavior.
  • The auto-calibration routine developed in June reduced setup time from 3 hours to 25 minutes per venue.

The platform is now robust enough for touring exhibitions and has been successfully deployed at two test venues without hardware failures. The remaining open question — sensor drift — is documented as an imponderable and will be monitored in future exhibitions.
