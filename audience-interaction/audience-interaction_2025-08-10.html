Conducted 12 semi-structured interviews with exhibition visitors who had spent at least 3 minutes with the breath-following sculpture. Selected quotes:

"At first I thought it was just doing its own thing, like a screensaver. But after a minute I realized it was responding to me. That moment of recognition was really special." — Visitor 4

"I felt like I was having a conversation with it, but without words. When I breathed slowly, it breathed slowly. When I moved closer, it got warmer. It was strangely intimate." — Visitor 7

"My daughter asked if it was alive. I didn't know what to say, because in some way it felt like it was." — Visitor 9

"I think I would have liked it more if I didn't know how it worked. The wall text explains too much. Sometimes mystery is better than understanding." — Visitor 11

The last comment connects directly to the transparency/enchantment tension identified earlier. There may be an argument for reducing or removing explanatory wall text and letting the experience speak for itself.
