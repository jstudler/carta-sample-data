First week of the "Responsive Environments" group show. Spent three afternoons observing visitor behavior around the breath-following sculpture in the alcove.

Interaction Patterns

Three distinct behavior patterns emerged:

  1. The Glancer (approx. 60% of visitors) — enters the alcove, watches the sculpture for 10-30 seconds, tries waving a hand near it, then moves on. These visitors typically do not discover the breath-following behavior.
  2. The Explorer (approx. 30%) — spends 1-3 minutes, tries different distances and movements, reads the wall text, and begins to understand the responsiveness. Some Explorers transition to the third pattern.
  3. The Contemplator (approx. 10%) — stands or sits quietly near the sculpture for 5-15 minutes, allowing the breath-following to synchronize. These visitors consistently report the most meaningful experiences in post-visit conversations.

Spatial Observations

The alcove placement works beautifully. Visitors naturally lower their voices upon entering, which reduces the ambient sound that would otherwise dominate the sensor readings. The transition from the bright main gallery to the dimmer alcove also functions as a perceptual threshold — visitors seem to shift into a more attentive mode.

One unexpected finding: groups of three or more rarely produce Contemplators. The social dynamics of a group visit seem to work against the sustained, quiet attention that the work rewards. Pairs are the optimal group size.
