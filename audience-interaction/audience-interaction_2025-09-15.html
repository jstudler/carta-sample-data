--- title: Future Prospects — Multi-Viewer Responsiveness date: 2025-09-15 topic: audience-interaction category: research type: lookout width: 2 ---

A compelling direction for future work: sculptures that respond to multiple viewers simultaneously.

The current system uses a single proximity sensor and a single microphone, which means it responds to the composite environment rather than to individual viewers. But what if a sculpture could distinguish between two or three nearby people and respond to each differently?

Technical possibilities:

  • An array of ToF sensors arranged radially could detect the positions of multiple viewers around a freestanding sculpture.
  • Beamforming microphone arrays could isolate the breathing rhythm of individual viewers.
  • Thermal imaging (e.g., the Lepton FLIR module) could provide rich, multi-person presence data at relatively low computational cost.

Artistic implications:

  • A sculpture that responds differently to each viewer creates a fundamentally different social situation. Each person's experience would be unique and potentially visible to the others.
  • The interplay between individual responses could create emergent patterns that none of the viewers individually control — the sculpture would become a kind of visual mediator between people.
  • This raises questions about privacy and consent that do not arise with the current single-sensor approach.

This direction would require significant technical development but could produce works that explore social dynamics rather than individual contemplation.
