--- title: Conclusion — Audience Interaction date: 2025-10-01 topic: audience-interaction category: exhibition type: conclusion width: 3 ---

The audience interaction research strand produced the richest insights of the entire project. The Galerie Meridian exhibition provided an invaluable opportunity to observe real viewer behavior over several weeks.

Key findings:

  • Only about 10% of visitors reach the contemplative engagement that the work is designed for. This is not necessarily a problem — the work is designed for depth rather than breadth.
  • Spatial context matters enormously. The alcove placement at Galerie Meridian produced significantly more extended engagement than the open-floor placement at earlier test events.
  • Pairs of visitors engage more deeply than solo visitors or groups, suggesting that a shared experience of responsiveness is more compelling than a solitary one.
  • Explanatory wall text may work against the experience. Several visitors preferred the mystery of not knowing how the system worked.

The transparency/enchantment tension has emerged as a central theme of the project. For future exhibitions, I plan to experiment with minimal or absent explanatory text, allowing the work to communicate through the experience itself.

The multi-viewer responsiveness direction identified as a lookout represents the most exciting future development path, bridging my current practice toward questions of social interaction and collective experience.
